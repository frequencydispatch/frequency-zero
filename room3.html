SUBJECT: Room designated "THIRD," relay structure, location withheld.

Floor plans filed with the original 1987 construction permit show a two-room structure: a transmission room and an equipment closet. No third room is indicated on any version of the blueprint, including the as-built revision filed in 1989.

Beginning approximately 1991, recovered transmissions began referencing a third room. The earliest reference is partial:

"...past the equipment closet there is a ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓ that was not there during the morning shift. ▓▓▓▓▓ no handle. no hinge. the third room has ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓..."

Three field reports describe entering the structure through the only known door and arriving in a room that does not match either listed space. Each report describes the room differently — dimensions, lighting, and contents are not consistent between accounts. One element recurs in every report without exception:

"there is no door behind you when you turn around."

A 1995 internal memo, partially recovered, instructs staff to stop discussing the third room over open channels. The memo itself was transmitted over an open channel. This was not flagged as an error at the time.

Photographic evidence does not exist. Every camera brought into the structure after 1991 returned either blank film, corrupted digital files, or images of the two known rooms only — timestamped correctly, showing no gap in time, and no record of a third space ever being entered.

"...if you are reading this and you remember ▓▓▓▓▓▓ you should stop. close this. do not ▓▓▓▓▓▓▓▓ it again. the room does not let you describe it correctly. it only lets you think you have..."
